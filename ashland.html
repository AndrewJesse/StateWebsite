About Ashland

Ashland, situated in Jackson County, Oregon, is located near the California border and is a key city in the Rogue Valley.

The city hosts Southern Oregon University and the renowned Oregon Shakespeare Festival, both crucial to its economy. Tourists are also drawn to its diverse eateries, galleries, and retail outlets. Notable attractions include the scenic Lithia Park along Ashland Creek and a convenient intercity bike trail.

Originally named "Ashland Mills," the city was inspired by Ashland County, Ohio, the hometown of its founder Abel Helman. Ashland is governed by a council-manager system and is known for its distinct liberal political stance in the southwest Oregon region.

Fun fact:
Lithia Park in Ashland, has drinking fountains that dispense water rich in lithium oxide. This safe and natural mineral gives the water a slightly effervescent taste.
